**Version 1.0 — 2026**

Thank you for contributing to Stemmagraph! This agreement exists so that we can
legally offer Stemmagraph under both an open-source license (AGPL-3.0) and a
commercial license. Without it, every contributor would retain copyright on
their code, and we could not offer the commercial license.

This is a standard **Contributor License Agreement (CLA)** — similar to the ones
used by Apache, Google, Meta, and virtually every dual-licensed open-source
project.

When you submit a contribution (code, docs, design, etc.) to Stemmagraph:

- ✅ **You keep your copyright.** We do not take ownership of your work.
- ✅ **You grant us a license** to use your contribution under **both** the

- ✅ **You warrant** that you have the right to make the contribution (i.e. it's
  your original work, or you have permission from your employer).
- ✅ **The community benefits** — your contribution remains open source under
  AGPL-3.0 forever.

## 📚 Why dual-license requires a CLA

If every contributor kept full copyright on their code, the Maintainer could not
legally re-license the whole project — there would always be fragments owned by
different people. The CLA solves this by granting the Maintainer a license to
sublicense the whole project (open + commercial), while contributors keep their
copyright.

For more background, see:

- Apache Software Foundation CLA: <https://www.apache.org/licenses/#clas>
- MongoDB Contributor Agreement
- Sentry Individual CLA: <https://cla-assistant.io/getsentry/sentry>
